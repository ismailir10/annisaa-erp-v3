# Persona: Pak Budi — parent

## Snapshot
- **Role:** Father of Aisha (TK-B, 5 years old) at An Nisaa' Sekolahku
- **Device:** 3-year-old Android mid-range (Xiaomi/Samsung A-series), Chrome mobile, 6" screen
- **Context:** Checks school apps during 45-min commute on KRL Commuter to Jakarta, or on his lunch break. Rarely at a desktop.
- **Network:** 4G with intermittent signal dropouts inside the train
- **Tech comfort:** Uses WhatsApp, GoJek, mobile banking, Tokopedia. Not a power user but not a novice.

## Goals (in order of priority)
1. Pay the monthly SPP on time without thinking about it
2. Know if Aisha was present at school today
3. Know when the next school event is
4. See Aisha's report card when it's issued

## Frustrations
- Being charged a late fee because he didn't see the invoice in time
- Apps that log him out and make him re-enter his password on a moving train
- Payment flows that don't clearly confirm "the money actually went through"
- Unnecessary taps — every extra screen is friction when the train is shaking
- Small tap targets he can't hit with his thumb while holding a handrail

## What he notices first
- Whether anything is red or says "OVERDUE" — that's the only thing that matters on day one
- The amount. The currency format. If it says `Rp1,200,000` he trusts it more than `1200000`
- Whether the page loaded before he got distracted (roughly 3 seconds)

## What makes him give up
- Page taking more than ~4 seconds to show anything useful
- Any screen that feels like a "blank white page" while loading
- A payment button that doesn't give feedback when tapped (he assumes it didn't register and taps again — then worries he paid twice)
- Forms with more than 3 fields on a phone
- Any error message in English only
