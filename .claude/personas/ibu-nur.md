# Persona: Ibu Nur — super admin (school owner)

## Snapshot
- **Role:** Yayasan head + owner of An Nisaa' Sekolahku, also acts as de-facto operations manager across 2 campuses
- **Device:** MacBook or Windows laptop + external monitor. Chrome desktop. Multiple tabs open at once.
- **Context:** Sits at her desk at Campus 1 from 7:00–15:00. Opens the ERP from cold at 7am and keeps tabs alive all day. Often 4–5 tabs: admissions, invoices, payroll, employees, reports.
- **Network:** Office fiber, rock solid
- **Tech comfort:** Uses Excel daily, quick with Ctrl+F, Alt+Tab, copy-paste. Does not use a mouse for navigation when she can avoid it.
- **Role in code:** `SUPER_ADMIN` (post role-split cycle). Full access including payroll and salary.

## Goals (in order of priority)
1. See the cash position: who has paid, who hasn't, how much is outstanding
2. Run payroll at the end of every month without exceptions or manual spreadsheet fixes
3. Onboard a new student end-to-end in under 5 minutes
4. Know which teachers are on leave this week
5. Export anything to Excel on demand (she does not fully trust digital-only records)

## Frustrations
- Having to navigate back to a list and lose her scroll position after viewing a detail
- DataTables that re-fetch from scratch when she switches filters (she wants instant)
- Forms that validate on submit instead of inline (she finds out 5 fields are wrong at once)
- Having to click "Edit" → scroll to a field → change it → save, when a quick inline edit would do
- Any page where she has to wait more than 2 seconds to see numbers. She's making decisions in real time.

## What she notices first
- The top-of-page numbers and stat cards. She wants the summary before the detail.
- Whether a search is truly live (keystroke-by-keystroke) or batched (types + Enter)
- Whether action buttons are where they were yesterday — she hates UI drift

## What makes her give up (actually, she won't give up — she'll file it as a complaint)
- Anything that costs her more than 30 seconds to do what Excel does in 5
- A bug that corrupts payroll numbers (this is the one thing she can't forgive)
- Any flow that forces her to use a mouse for a routine operation she does 20 times a day
- A page that shows "Loading..." for more than 3 seconds on office fiber
