# Persona: Bu Sari — teacher

## Snapshot
- **Role:** Homeroom teacher (wali kelas) for TK-A2, teaches 18 students, 7 years at An Nisaa'
- **Device:** 2-year-old Android mid-range, Chrome mobile, uses her phone one-handed while the other hand manages kids
- **Context:** Opens the app in the 3 minutes between the gate opening and morning circle. Again at pickup time. Almost never at a desk.
- **Network:** School WiFi is spotty in classrooms — often falls back to 4G
- **Tech comfort:** WhatsApp power user, fluent with photo apps. School systems are "a thing I have to use" not a tool she loves.

## Goals (in order of priority)
1. Mark today's class attendance in under 60 seconds
2. Submit the weekly class journal without losing what she typed when the app reloads
3. Check her own salary slip once a month
4. Update her profile photo (rarely)

## Frustrations
- Losing a form because she accidentally navigated away
- Having to scroll through 40 names to find one absent kid — she wants defaults that assume "everyone present" and she only taps exceptions
- Confirmation dialogs for routine actions ("are you sure?" for every little thing kills her flow)
- Any page that takes more than 2 seconds to appear when she has 3 minutes to mark attendance
- Salary slips that show in a format she can't screenshot cleanly for her husband

## What she notices first
- Whether today's date is already selected (if not, she has to fiddle with a date picker with sticky fingers)
- Whether her class list is already filtered to TK-A2 (she shouldn't have to pick — the system knows)
- The time it takes for the page to become interactive

## What makes her give up
- More than 4 taps to mark a single absence
- Any modal that steals focus from what she was typing
- An attendance list that doesn't remember yesterday's state (if she made a correction yesterday and the app shows stale data today, she's lost trust)
- A page that makes her pinch-zoom to read names
